How many Bitcoin should your country own?

Based on Population

Simplifying slightly, assuming all 21M Bitcoin are mined, and none are lost, the number of Bitcoin each country should have, (or held by it's population), is shown below.

There are a few countries right now sitting quite comfortably, in a Bitcoin economy they could be catapulted from one of the poorest to riches countries in the world. Others will potentially have to pay more to catchup later or continue to fight to maintain the current system. As the efficiencies of the Bitcoin economy become more and more apparent the cost to maintain and compete using the existing model will become more and more costly. There are multiple ways a country could end up in a stronger position through Bitcoin.

  • The government themselves hold Bitcoin.
  • Bitcoin regulation encourages development of infrastructure and a mindset of holding some of your wealth in Bitcoin.
  • Government controls, restrictions and currency instability highlight the need for Bitcoin and drive adoption.

All three are happening right now, and at different rates across the globe. In a way, some of the most stable countries could end up being harmed the most by this as their populations are the slowest to adapt as the need for change is lowest.
